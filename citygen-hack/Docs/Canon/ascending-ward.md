# The Ascending Ward

## Core Identity

- Officially, the city is a sacred hospital for healing pilgrims.
- Actually, it is a bureaucracy that turns patients and residents into managed labor and biological output.
- Historically, it began as a temple-healing site around a vertical anomaly.
- Emergent behavior: it is becoming a living megastructure that grows new districts from ritual, waste, and suffering.

## Origin

- Founding catalyst: a sacred healing machine, spring, wound, or organ in the inner core.
- First institution: a priest-healer order.
- First settlement logic: public pilgrims moved through ceremonial routes while staff used hidden core paths.
- Oldest surviving structures: temple stairs, spring chambers, ritual halls, reliquary vaults.

## Vertical Logic

- Lower levels: waste, labor intake, quarantine, forgotten temple ruins.
- Middle levels: public wards, clinics, housing, markets, food, worship.
- Upper levels: administration, elite recovery, research, military control.
- Core: utilities, restricted transport, ancient healing machinery, extraction systems.
- Outer shell: public life, balconies, shrines, visible circulation.
- Crown: unstable organic growth and parasitic expansion.

## Main Contradictions

- Healing vs extraction.
- Worship vs bureaucracy.
- Public sanctuary vs coercive labor.
- Medical care vs military experimentation.
- Planned architecture vs living growth.

## Navigation Anchors

- The Healing Atrium
- The Votive Surgery Theater

- The Military Recovery Garden
- The Growth Crown

## District Design Rule

Every district should define:

- an official function
- a hidden function
- an inherited function

Example:

- Healing Atrium: public hospital lobby / patient sorting / former temple courtyard
- Labor Wards: rehabilitation housing / forced labor / former pilgrim dormitory
- Root Cistern: water purification / biological waste processing / sacred spring
